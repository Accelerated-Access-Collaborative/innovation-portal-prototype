Medicines

Clinical effectiveness

The analysis of clinical effectiveness must be based on data from all relevant studies of the best available quality and should consider:

  • the range of typical patients
  • normal clinical circumstances
  • clinically relevant outcomes
  • comparison with relevant comparators
  • measures of both relative and absolute effectiveness with appropriate measures of uncertainty

Evidence of various types and from multiple sources may inform a NICE assessment of medicinal products evaluated through the Technology Appraisal or Highly Specialised Technologies Process. Evidence types may include randomised controlled trials (RCTs), non-randomised and non-controlled evidence as well as qualitative research.

Economic evidence

The preferred form of economic evaluation of a medicine is a cost-effectiveness (specifically cost-utility) analysis. This type of economic analysis seeks to establish whether differences in expected costs between options can be justified in terms of changes in expected health effects.

Key information underpinning a cost-utility analysis (CUA) includes data on clinical outcomes, health-related quality of life, costs and resource use, all of which are informed by different types of evidence.

In addition, companies will need to provide a budget impact analysis to show the financial impact of their medicinal product over the first 3 years of its use in the NHS.

Where to go for more information

The following resources provide more information on the types and sources of evidence required to assess the clinical and cost effectiveness of medicinal products.

  • For the majority of medicines and therapeutics: The Guide to the methods of technology appraisal 2013 (sections 3 and 5)
  • For highly specialised technologies: Interim Process and Methods of the Highly Specialised Technologies Programme
  • For detailed technical guides: NICE’s Decision Support Unit have developed a suite of Technical Support Documents providing guidance on methodological approaches to consider when preparing evidence for NICE assessment
  • For more information on the Budget Impact Test

Bespoke support for developing your clinical and economic evidence case is available through NICE Scientific Advice.
