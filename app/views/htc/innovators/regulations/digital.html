Digital products and services Regulations

{{ contentsList({ items: [ { href: "/htc/innovators/regulations", text: "Medicines and medical devices" }, { href: "/htc/innovators/regulations/digital", text: "Digital products, services and components", current: "true" }, { href: "/htc/innovators/regulations/surgical", text: "Surgical procedures and techniques" }, { href: "/htc/innovators/regulations/other", text: "Models of care and other innovations" } ] }) }}

Some digital health technologies are classed as medical devices and therefore are within remit for CE marking under the Medical Devices Regulation.

For digital health technologies that are not classed as medical devices, there is currently no established safety regulatory framework, but the NICE Evidence Standards Framework, NHS Digital Health Technology Standard and the guide from Public Health England on Evaluating Digital Health Products may provide detailed guidance on standards they need to meet for adoption within the NHS.

Note that the Government Service Manual, or for NHS services and products, the NHS Service Manual apply to both clinical and non-clinical products and services, including digital components of other types of innovation.
